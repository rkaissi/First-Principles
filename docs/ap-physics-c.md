# AP Physics C — calculus-first prep & game map

**AP Physics C** is a **calculus-based** sequence (typically **Mechanics** and **Electricity & Magnetism** as separate courses/exams). You are expected to use **\(dx\), \(dv\), \(dt\)**, **integrals for accumulation**, and **vector forms** where appropriate—**not** only memorized constant-acceleration shortcuts.

This page is an **unofficial** **concept map** and a link to **First Principles** levels that illustrate **calculus stories** (graphs as functions of a variable). It is **not** a full Physics C textbook.

---

## Mechanics — calculus trilogy

| Idea | Calculus form | Notes |
|------|----------------|------|
| **Kinematics** | \(\vec v = d\vec r/dt\), \(\vec a = d\vec v/dt\) | Area under \(a(t)\) ⇄ \(\Delta v\); area under \(v(t)\) ⇄ \(\Delta x\) |
| **Newton’s second law** | \(\sum \vec F = d\vec p/dt\) | Often \(m\vec a\) when mass is constant |
| **Work–energy** | \(W = \int \vec F \cdot d\vec r\) | Leads to kinetic energy theorems for conservative forces |
| **Rotation** | \(\tau = d\mathbf L/dt\) | For fixed axis: \(\mathbf L = I\boldsymbol\omega\) |

**Angular momentum:** \(\mathbf L = \mathbf r \times \mathbf p\); magnitude forms include \(L = I\omega\) for symmetric spin about a fixed axis. **Conservation** when net **external torque** about a point/axis is zero—classic “skater pulls arms in” \(\Rightarrow\) \(\omega\) increases if \(I\) drops.

## E&M (Physics C: Electricity & Magnetism) — integral forms

You routinely meet **Gauss’s law**, **Ampère’s law**, and concepts built on **flux** and **line integrals**—the exam expects you to **set up** \(\int \vec E \cdot d\vec A\) style reasoning on symmetric geometries (not necessarily the messiest anti-closed form).

## Game levels that echo Physics C *calculus vibes*

These are **motifs**—visual calculus hooks, not a full lab course:

| Theme | Example stages |
|--------|----------------|
| **Exponential decay** | *Physics C: exponential decay (τ, RC)* — \(e^{-t/\tau}\) solutions |
| **Projectile / kinematics** | *Physics C: projectile height y(t)* — parabola in time |
| **Rotation / angular momentum** | *Physics C: angular momentum & \(L=I\omega\)* — SHM framing |
| **Oscillations** | Sine/cosine levels; damped engineering stage for lossy oscillators |

Pair with **AP Calculus BC** prep for **parametric motion** language (\(x(t), y(t)\)) if you study motion in 2D.
