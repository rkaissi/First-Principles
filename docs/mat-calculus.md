# MAT — calculus & reasoning (prep notes)

The **Mathematics Admissions Test (MAT)** is used (notably by **Oxford**, and some other UK courses) to assess **mathematical thinking** with **careful reasoning** and **multi-step problems**. The **format and timing** have evolved (including computer-based delivery in recent cycles)—always check **Oxford’s official MAT page** for the current specification.

This page is an **unofficial** calculus-aligned **topic lens** and **study habits** note. It does **not** reproduce MAT questions (copyright)—use **official past papers and materials** for authentic practice.

---

## How calculus tends to appear on MAT-style work

- **Algebra first**: many items reward **exact manipulation** before you “see” a derivative or integral.
- **Graphical reasoning**: sketch or interpret **increasing/decreasing** behaviour, **turning points**, **asymptotes**, **domains** (\(\ln x\), \(\sqrt{x}\), \(1/x\)).
- **Existence & uniqueness moods**: “how many solutions?” often uses **monotonicity** of \(f'\) or **IFT-style** thinking without naming theorems.
- **Proof fragments**: show an inequality, or explain why a configuration is impossible—**calculus is a tool**, not always the whole task.

---

## Calculus topic checklist (MAT-relevant)

### Differentiation

- **Standard rules**: linearity, **product**, **quotient**, **chain**.
- **Implicit differentiation** (curves not solved as \(y=f(x)\); circles, related setups).
- **Second derivative** and **concavity** / inflection (where smooth).

### Integration

- **Antiderivatives** of polynomials, \(1/x\), \(e^{kx}\), \(\sin/\cos\) with constants.
- **Definite integrals** as **signed area**; **split** where the function crosses the axis.
- **Recognition** of derivative-inside-integral (**reverse chain rule**) patterns.

### Functions & traps

- **Exponentials vs polynomials** for growth; **logarithms** for slow growth and domain \(x>0\).
- **Modulus** \(|x|\): different behaviour left/right of a kink.
- **Rational functions**: poles, sign either side of vertical asymptotes.

### (Broader) links MAT students train alongside calculus

- **Sequences & series** intuition (partial sums, “does it settle?”).
- **Coordinate geometry** (circles, intersections)—often paired with **simultaneous** algebra.

## Question *habits* (not past papers)

- **Breathe after reading**: classify the object (function, graph, inequality, counting solutions).
- **Sanity-check dimensions / limits**: as \(x\to\infty\), as \(x\to 0^+\), at **endpoints** of an interval.
- **Avoid “magic calculus”**: if differentiation gives a mess, try **substitution** or **rewrite** the problem.

---

## Practice workflow

1. **Official MAT resources** + **timed practice** under exam rules.
2. **Annotate errors** by type: **algebra slip vs concept vs graph misread**.
3. Use **First Principles** only as a **visual derivative/area** supplement—not a substitute for MAT-style written reasoning.
